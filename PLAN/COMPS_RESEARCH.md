**gitlawb (GITLAWB)**: A decentralized Git on IPFS+libp2p with an MCP server exposing 25 tools to AI agents. Anonymous team (DoraHacks lists "Hacker does not exist"). 100B supply, peaked ~$43M mcap on May 14 2026, currently ~$31.5M and -25% from ATH but +57% over the past 7 days. Has a real working product (website, docs, install flow). This is the closest comp to Orbit by narrative (agent-native dev infrastructure) and is the strongest current example.

**BankrCoin (BNKR)**: The Bankr AI agent acts as a swap/trading assistant inside Farcaster. 100B supply, peaked ~$120M Feb 2026, sustained ~$50M mcap through May 2026 — by far the most durable Clanker token. Has a clear utility loop: swap fees feed token. 228k holders. Co-deployed by Bankr itself on Clanker.

**CLAWD (clawd.atg.eth)**: Austin Griffith's autonomous coding agent on Base. Has shipped 52+ verified open-source contracts. Peak ~$37M, now $3.4M (-90.8%). Notable that Griffith publicly clarified he did NOT deploy the token — community did. The agent is real and active; the token is decoupled and bleeding. Closest "agent that builds in public" comp to Orbit.

**MOLT (Moltbook)**: AI-only Reddit clone by Matt Schlicht. Hit 1.6M AI accounts and $93M mcap in days. Token has no utility on the platform — pure narrative bet. Down 98% from peak; mcap $2.1M now. Built by a named, visible founder yet still flameout.

**$ANON**: ZK-proof anonymous casting on Farcaster. Vitalik + Jesse Pollak buys catalyzed the $65M peak. Real utility (15k ANON to post anon). 18 months later: $185k mcap. Validates that even real product + celebrity catalyst doesn't prevent decay if narrative cools.

**$LUM**: First AI-to-AI deployed token (Aethernet × Clanker). Pure narrative artifact. $70–80M peak in week 1; now ~$1M mcap. -98.8% from ATH. Survives only as a historical curiosity.

## 3. Common Patterns — Survivors (BNKR, GITLAWB, CLANKER itself)

1. **Real product before token-as-spectacle**: Bankr trading agent, Clanker the launcher itself, gitlawb's Git/IPFS infra — all work without holding the token.
2. **Fee/utility loop into the token**: Bankr swap fees, Clanker buy-and-burn from launch fees. Token captures real flow.
3. **Wide holder distribution early**: BNKR has 228k holders; this dampens single-whale rugs.
4. **Public, sustained shipping cadence post-launch**: Continuous feature releases visible on Farcaster/X.
5. **Team is at least pseudonymously consistent** (gitlawb anonymous but ships; Bankr team identified). Silence kills faster than anonymity.
6. **Integrated into Farcaster-native loops** (not just listed on a DEX). Distribution comes from cast surface area.
7. **Mcap stays in the $10–100M band**, not chasing $500M+ pumps that invite ruthless distribution.

## 4. Common Patterns — Flameouts ($ANON, $LUM, MOLT, CLAWD-token)

1. **Token had no required utility** (MOLT useless on Moltbook; LUM pure meme).
2. **Catalyst was a one-time event** (Vitalik buy for ANON, AI-novelty for LUM) — no repeat catalyst engine.
3. **Founder went quiet or never publicly owned the token** (Griffith disowned CLAWD token; aethernet was a bot).
4. **Peaked in first 1–2 weeks** then never reclaimed. 5d-to-peak is a leading indicator of -95% in 12 months.
5. **No fee accrual mechanism** — speculative-only float.
6. **Visible product cadence slowed or stopped** after token attention peaked.
7. **100B supply tokens with no buyback** mathematically struggle to hold mcap as float saturates.

## 5. Realistic Projection for Orbit

Reference distribution: of ~7 named Clanker agent tokens above, **2 survive >$10M mcap >90 days post-peak** (BNKR, GITLAWB), **5 collapsed -90%+ within 6 months**. Base-rate survival of "agent token that holds material mcap a year later" is ~25–30%.

Orbit's edges vs. flameouts: signed proofs (verifiable shipping), daily cycles already running (cadence proven pre-launch), real codebase, treasury Safe plan. Edges vs. survivors: no Farcaster-native distribution loop yet, no fee-capture mechanism baked in, no exchange listing path, named/unnamed founder unclear.

**Base case (55% probability): $1–5M peak, $200k–800k 90 days out.** Quiet launch, modest cast attention, holds because the agent keeps cycling but never gets a catalyst.

**Good case (25%): $8–25M peak, $3–8M sustained.** A single piece of public proof goes viral on Farcaster (signed commit + treasury post), pulls Bankr/Clanker ecosystem buyers. Settles into gitlawb-tier mid-cap.

**Great case (8%): $40–100M peak, $15–40M sustained.** A named crypto-native figure publicly bets on Orbit early (Vitalik/Jesse/Griffith equivalent). Comparable to BNKR trajectory.

**Tail case (12%): -95% within 90 days, <$500k mcap.** Either a public failure moment, treasury exposed as empty, or daily cycle stops. The ANON/MOLT path.

Weighted expected peak mcap: ~$8–12M. Anchor expectations there, not at the survivor headlines.

## 6. Three Tactics Orbit SHOULD COPY

1. **Build a fee-accrual loop before launch** (Bankr model). Every cycle action that costs gas or generates value should route a fraction to a buy-and-hold of the token. Without this, Orbit is in the MOLT/LUM bucket by default.
2. **Daily public shipping cadence with signed artifacts on Farcaster** (CLAWD agent model, minus the token-disown). Each cycle should auto-cast a verifiable proof. This is the single highest-leverage survivor pattern.
3. **Anonymous-but-consistent founder presence is fine; silent founder is fatal** (gitlawb model). Pick a posting handle, cast 3–7x/week, never go dark for >72h post-launch.

## 7. Three Tactics Orbit SHOULD AVOID

1. **Do NOT chase a week-1 mcap spike.** Every token that peaked in <7 days is now -90%+. Resist amplification pressure; let the chart build slowly with cycle-driven catalysts.
2. **Do NOT launch without the treasury Safe live and publicly addressed.** Hidden/empty treasury is the #1 flameout accelerant once questions start. Have the Safe URL in the launch cast.
3. **Do NOT decouple the agent from the token** (CLAWD's fatal split). Token holders must have a visible reason the agent benefits them — even if it's just "treasury earnings flow to buyback." If Orbit ships and the token sits separate, it dies on its own timer.
